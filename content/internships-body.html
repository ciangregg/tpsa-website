Hamilton

Run by the School of Maths in Trinity, this is a six week research project. The professor you must find to supervise the project does not have to be from the School of Maths, but the project topic must have sufficient mathematics content. The research project can be done alone, or in groups of two or three. Financial support starts from €675 per person, depending on the group size.

SURE

The Summer Undergraduate Research Experience is a 10-week internship through the School of Physics. Seven positions are offered yearly with a €300/week stipend and flexible dates. Open to Junior Sophisters considering postgraduate research. Fourteen projects are available (4 Experimental, 8 Computational, 2 Theoretical). Applications open in early February and close late February. Selection is based mainly on academic performance and a one-page application outlining background, goals, and project preferences.

Laidlaw Scholarship

A two-year programme combining undergraduate research and leadership training. Open to 2nd-year (4-year courses) or 2nd/3rd-year (5-year courses) students. Summer 1: six-week self-designed research project (€3,500). Summer 2: six-week international leadership placement (€3,500). Includes training days, a conference, and up to €1,900 for research or travel. Around 25 scholarships are awarded annually. Applications due early February, requiring a video, research and leadership proposals, and supervisor support.

DIAS Summer Studentship (Theoretical Physics)

The Dublin Institute for Advanced Studies offers 2-month summer studentships in its School of Theoretical Physics, starting in early June. Eleven students are selected to work on topics such as quantum state transfer, matrix models, and causal set theory. The stipend is €3,000 (€1,500/month). Applications typically open in spring and require a CV, personal statement, and confirmation of eligibility for first-class honours.

Tyndall Summer Fellowship

The Tyndall National Institute in Cork offers a 12-week paid summer fellowship starting in early June. Students work on computational research projects in areas such as nanotechnology, photonics, and ICT, under the supervision of professional researchers. The programme includes workshops in Python, MATLAB, dissemination, networking, and laser building, and concludes with a poster presentation. Applications open in December and close in late January, with interviews held in mid-to-late February and offers made by mid-to-late March. A CV, short statement, and questionnaire are required (no references). There is potential to work towards publication.

CERN

CERN offers an 8-13 week summer programme (June-September) for Bachelor's or Master's students (not PhD) in physics, engineering, computer science, or mathematics. Students work full-time on a research project in their field and submit a report, with optional poster or public presentation. The programme includes lectures, workshops, tours, and time to explore Switzerland and France. Participants receive ~93 CHF/day, travel reimbursement (~500 CHF from Dublin), full medical insurance, and subsidised accommodation near CERN. Applications close ~31st January. Highly competitive (~2% acceptance). Requires CV, transcript, two references, and responses on motivation, relevant experience, and interests.

DESY

DESY (Deutsches Elektronen-Synchrotron) offers a 7.5-week summer programme for undergraduate students in physics running from from late July to early September. It gives students hands on research experience in a large international labaoratory. Projects are available in photon science, elementary particle physics & accelerators and astroparticle physics. Requires CV, transcript, two references, a 1000-character motivation statement, and ranked project preferences (e.g. experimental/theoretical, particle/photon/astroparticle research). Decisions are announced by early April.

The Rudolf Peierls Centre for Theoretical Physics and other groups within Oxford Department of Physics run summer internships. Applications for summer 2026 will open in January/February 2026 with likely due date of mid-March 2026. Applicants asked to submit one-page application and academic reference letter. Must have right to work in UK to apply. Other Oxford physics groups also run similar internships including AOPP (Atmospheric, Oceanic and Planetary Physics) and UROP (Undergraduate Research Opportunity Programme) across various departments. UROP duration typically 6-8 weeks during summer vacation. Students receive bursary for their work. Open to Oxford undergraduates and students from other institutions. Projects available in physics, engineering, and related sciences. Requires academic transcript and project proposal.

RISE

RISE (Research Internships in Science and Engineering) Germany offers summer research internships for undergraduate students in physics, engineering, and related fields. The three month paid internship takes place at a top German research institutions or university.

SETI

The Breakthrough Listen Undergraduate Research Internship offers an 8-10 week paid position in SETI research, working with Prof. Evan Keane (Head of I-LOFAR). The programme includes lectures from UC Berkeley on astrophysics, Linux servers, Git, radio telescopes, and pulsars/FRBs. The work is programming-intensive, involving Linux, Docker, and data analysis; experience with Python or similar is highly recommended. Open to undergraduate and Master's students. Stipend: €600/week. Applications typically close by February 1st and include an interview. Requires CV, transcript, cover letter, and a reference. Applicants should express interest in SETI and their motivation for applying.
