Measuring the Strength of the Strong Nuclear Force

In this video, Professor Stefan Sint provides an in-depth overview of key topics related to measuring the strong nuclear force. This talk unpacks the theory, tools, and ongoing questions physicists face in decoding the strong interaction.

What Quantum Theories Teach us About Gravity

In this video, Professor Manuela Kulaxizi tells us what we can learn about gravity using quantum theories. Enjoy the exploration from the smallest parts of the universe to the largest!

Simulating Stochastic Processes with Quantum Physics

In this TPSA talk, Professor Felix Binder explains how to simulate stochastic processes with quantum physics. Please enjoy!

A Theory of Everything Integrable: Self-Dual Yang-Mills

This collaboration between the TPSA and DU Mathsoc is a talk by Professor Tristan McLoughlin on self-dual Yang-Mills theory, and how it is a potential theory of everything (integrable). Please enjoy!

Extreme Explosions and Catastrophic Collisions

This TPSA talk by Professor Kate Maguire is about extreme explosions and catastrophic collisions in the universe. Topics discussed include different types of supernovae, as well as gravitational waves and methods astrophysicists use to detect them. Please enjoy!

Investigating Quark Confinement by QCD on the Computer

This TPSA talk by Professor Mike Peardon is about using high performance computing to investigate lattice QCD problems. Please enjoy!

From Birr to Boolardy: The Universe at 1000 FPS

This TPSA talk by Professor Evan Keane is about the universe at 1000 frames per second, and how this can be used to study astrophysical phenomena. Please enjoy!

Black Hole Entropy in Supergravity

The TPSA talk by Prof. Manya Sahni is about black hole entropy in supergravity. Please enjoy!
